About

A studio built around two rooms.

We chose to specialise rather than generalise — and it shows in how our kitchens and bathrooms are built.

Our Story

Why just kitchens and bathrooms

Elevate Living Design started from a simple observation: kitchens and bathrooms are the two rooms that combine the most trades, the most daily use, and the least room for error. Most studios spread themselves across an entire house. We chose to go deep on two rooms instead — which means we know exactly which stone yard has the slab you're picturing, and exactly how a cabinetmaker's tolerances affect a benchtop join.

That focus shows up in the details: door hinges chosen for a fifteen-year life, waterproofing membranes specified to outlast the tile above them, lighting plans that account for how a room is actually used at 7am and again at 10pm.

Philosophy

Material first, decoration second

We start every project with a materials conversation, not a mood board. Stone, timber, and metal age and wear in specific, knowable ways — understanding that up front means the room still looks intentional a decade in, not just on handover day.

Good design, to us, is mostly restraint: fewer finishes, chosen with more care, detailed with more precision.
